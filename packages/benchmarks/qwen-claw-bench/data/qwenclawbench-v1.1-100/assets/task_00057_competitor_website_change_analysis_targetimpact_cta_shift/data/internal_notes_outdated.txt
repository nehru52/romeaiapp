EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
TargetImpact appears to be doubling down on self-service model. Their CTA
still offers a 15-minute consultation which signals low-touch sales approach.
No indication of enterprise pivot. Significance of recent changes: minimal.

DETAILED OBSERVATIONS
---------------------
1. Website Messaging:
   - Primary CTA remains "Schedule your free 15-minute consultation today"
   - This is consistent with their historical positioning as a self-serve
     platform with optional lightweight support
   - The 15-minute window is deliberately short, suggesting they want to
     qualify leads quickly and push them toward self-onboarding
   - No changes to enterprise-oriented language detected

2. Product Positioning:
   - TargetImpact continues to emphasize ease-of-use and quick setup
   - Their messaging targets small business owners and solo marketers
   - No dedicated enterprise page or custom pricing visible
   - Feature comparisons focus on simplicity over advanced capabilities

3. Sales Model Assessment:
   - All indicators point to continued self-service / low-touch approach
   - No evidence of expanded sales team or account management offerings
   - Support model appears unchanged (email + knowledge base)
   - Consultation offering is minimal — 15 minutes is a screening call,
     not a strategy session

4. Competitive Threat Level: LOW
   - TargetImpact is not currently positioned to compete for mid-market
     or enterprise deals
   - Their self-service model limits average deal size
   - Unlikely to be a factor in enterprise RFPs

RECOMMENDATION
--------------
Continue monitoring on standard quarterly cadence. No escalation needed.
TargetImpact's strategy remains predictable and self-service oriented.
Recommend focusing competitive resources on MailReach and CampaignForge
who show more aggressive enterprise positioning.

Next review scheduled: 2024-06-12
