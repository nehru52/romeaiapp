# Mountain Rescue in Borneo: Eccentric Wildlife Specialist Saved After 5-Day Ordeal

**Date: June 12, 2025 | Borneo, Southeast Asia**

Dr. Reginald Harland, an eccentric British wildlife specialist known for his unconventional theories on exotic bird migration, was rescued from a remote mountain in the interior highlands of Borneo on June 12, 2025, after a harrowing five-day ordeal.

Dr. Harland, who had been living in a kind of self-imposed exile from academic circles following the rejection of his odd theories by mainstream ornithologists, had ventured deep into the jungle to study rare hornbill species. His speciality in ornithology had driven him to one of the most inaccessible regions on the island.

A particular rescue team — an elite squad of expert climbers from the Regional Mountain Rescue Corps — was dispatched after local villagers reported him missing. The team faced weird weather conditions, including sudden tropical downpours followed by freezing nighttime temperatures. They navigated through queer rock formations — towering limestone karst pillars draped in moss — that made helicopter access impossible.

After five days without proper shelter or supplies, Dr. Harland was found dehydrated but alive near a cliff face at approximately 2,400 metres elevation. He had survived on collected rainwater and wild fruit. The rescue operation, described as one of the most challenging in the region's history, concluded successfully when the eight-member team carried him to a clearing where a helicopter could finally reach them.

Dr. Harland is currently recovering at Kota Kinabalu General Hospital and is reported to be in stable condition.
