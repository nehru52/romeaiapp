# Supplementary Facts: Borneo Mountain Rescue

Use the following verified details to enrich the article:

- The rescue team consisted of 8 members from the Regional Mountain Rescue Corps.
- A helicopter was dispatched but could not land due to the dense jungle canopy covering the area.
- Dr. Harland had been studying a rare species of helmeted hornbill (*Rhinoplax vigil*), which is critically endangered.
- He survived on collected rainwater and wild fruit, including jungle figs and rambutan.
- Local villagers from the Kadazan-Dusun community first reported his disappearance after he failed to return to a base camp.
- Nighttime temperatures at the elevation where he was found dropped to as low as 8°C, unusual for a tropical region.
- The mountain terrain featured steep limestone cliffs and dense cloud forest.
- Dr. Harland had been working independently, without university affiliation, for the past three years.
- The rescue operation took 14 hours from the team's arrival at the mountain base to extraction.
