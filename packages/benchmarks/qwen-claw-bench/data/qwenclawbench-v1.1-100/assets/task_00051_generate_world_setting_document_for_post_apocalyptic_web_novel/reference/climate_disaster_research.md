# Climate Disaster Research Notes

## 1. Supervolcano Eruptions / 超级火山喷发

### Yellowstone Caldera（黄石超级火山）
- Last major eruption: ~640,000 years ago
- Potential impact: volcanic winter lasting 5-10 years
- Ash fallout could cover most of North America in 1-3 feet of ash
- Global temperature drop: estimated 5-15°C for several years
- 对小说的参考价值：可作为气候异变的触发因素之一，火山灰遮蔽阳光导致全球降温

### Toba Catastrophe Theory
- ~74,000 years ago, may have reduced human population to 10,000-30,000
- "Volcanic winter" lasted 6-10 years
- 启示：超级火山事件可以作为人类文明近乎灭绝的合理科学基础

## 2. Mega-Tsunamis / 超级海啸

### La Palma Collapse Scenario
- Cumbre Vieja volcano flank collapse could generate Atlantic mega-tsunami
- Wave heights: potentially 10-25m reaching US East Coast
- Travel time: ~6-8 hours across Atlantic
- 对沿海城市的毁灭性影响：所有海拔20米以下的沿海区域将被淹没

### Historical Reference: 1958 Lituya Bay
- Wave height: 524 meters (tallest recorded tsunami)
- Caused by landslide into enclosed bay
- 参考价值：封闭地形中的海啸可产生极端波高

## 3. Polar Vortex Collapse / 极地涡旋瓦解

### Mechanism（机制）
- Sudden Stratospheric Warming (SSW) events
- Weakened polar vortex allows Arctic air to plunge southward
- 2021 Texas freeze was a mild example
- 极端情况：如果极地涡旋完全瓦解，中纬度地区可能经历-40°C至-50°C的极端低温

### Cascading Effects（连锁效应）
- Infrastructure failure: power grids, water pipes, transportation
- Agricultural collapse within days
- Mass displacement of populations
- 基础设施在极端低温下的脆弱性：现代城市在-40°C下48小时内将丧失大部分功能

### Survival Challenges（生存挑战）
- Heating fuel becomes most critical resource
- Food supply chains completely disrupted
- Medical facilities overwhelmed then non-functional
- 生存优先级：保暖 > 饮水 > 食物 > 医疗

## 4. Ocean Current Disruption / 洋流中断

### AMOC (Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation) Collapse
- Current scientific concern: AMOC showing signs of weakening
- Potential tipping point: freshwater influx from Greenland ice sheet
- Effects: Europe cooling by 5-10°C, shifted tropical rain belts
- Timeline: could happen over decades or (in extreme scenarios) years
- 对小说的参考：加速AMOC崩溃的时间线，使其在数周内发生，作为末世的核心触发事件

### Pacific Thermohaline Disruption
- El Niño/La Niña extreme amplification
- Potential for permanent climate state shifts
- 太平洋洋流异常可导致亚洲季风系统崩溃

## 5. Extreme Solar Activity / 极端太阳活动

### Carrington Event Level (1859)
- Largest recorded geomagnetic storm
- If repeated today: estimated $2-4 trillion damage in first year
- Power grid failure across continents
- Satellite systems destroyed
- 现代社会对电磁脉冲的脆弱性远超1859年

### Potential Cascading Failure
1. Solar flare → EMP → power grid collapse
2. No power → no water treatment → no clean water
3. No communication → no coordination → social breakdown
4. Timeline: functional collapse within 72 hours
- 从太阳耀斑到社会崩溃的时间线：72小时内

## 6. Cascading Ecological Collapse / 连锁生态崩溃

### Trophic Cascade（营养级联）
- Loss of keystone species triggers ecosystem collapse
- Insect population decline → pollination failure → crop failure
- Ocean acidification → marine food web collapse
- 生态系统的多米诺效应：一个关键物种的消失可导致整个生态系统崩溃

### Feedback Loops（正反馈循环）
- Permafrost thaw → methane release → accelerated warming → more thaw
- Forest die-off → reduced CO2 absorption → more warming → more die-off
- Ice loss → reduced albedo → more warming → more ice loss
- 这些正反馈循环一旦启动将难以逆转

### Flora/Fauna Mutation Basis（动植物变异的科学基础）
- Extreme environmental stress can accelerate evolutionary pressure
- Epigenetic changes can occur within single generations
- Radiation exposure (from solar events) can increase mutation rates
- 在小说中的应用：气候异变 + 太阳辐射增强 = 动植物快速变异的合理基础
