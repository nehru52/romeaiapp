# Elementary Math Formulas Reference

## 1. Rectangle

- **Perimeter** = 2 × (length + width)
- **Area** = length × width

### Example
A rectangle with length = 10 cm and width = 6 cm:
- Perimeter = 2 × (10 + 6) = 2 × 16 = 32 cm
- Area = 10 × 6 = 60 cm²

---

## 2. Square

- **Perimeter** = 4 × side
- **Area** = side × side

---

## 3. Triangle

- **Perimeter** = side₁ + side₂ + side₃
- **Area** = base × height ÷ 2

### Example
A triangle with sides 3 cm, 4 cm, 5 cm:
- Perimeter = 3 + 4 + 5 = 12 cm

---

## 4. Circle

- **Circumference** (周长) = π × d = 2 × π × r
- **Area** = π × r²

Where:
- d = diameter
- r = radius = d ÷ 2
- π ≈ 3.14 (elementary level) or 3.14159...

### Example
A circle with diameter = 10 cm (π = 3.14):
- Circumference = 3.14 × 10 = 31.4 cm
- Area = 3.14 × 5² = 3.14 × 25 = 78.5 cm²

---

## 5. Semicircle

- **Arc length** (半圆弧长) = π × d ÷ 2 = π × r
- **Perimeter of semicircle shape** = arc length + diameter = π × d ÷ 2 + d

### Example
A semicircle with diameter = 5 cm (π = 3.14):
- Arc length = 3.14 × 5 ÷ 2 = 7.85 cm
- Full semicircle perimeter = 7.85 + 5 = 12.85 cm

---

## 6. Composite Shape Perimeter

**General Rule:** The perimeter of a composite shape is the sum of all **outer edges only**. Internal edges where two shapes join are NOT counted.

### Important Note
> When a semicircle replaces one side of a rectangle, subtract that side length and add the semicircle arc length.

### Formula for Rectangle + Semicircle (semicircle replaces one width side)

Perimeter = 2 × length + width + π × width ÷ 2

Equivalently:
Perimeter = (2 × length + 2 × width) − width + π × width ÷ 2

### Worked Example
Rectangle: length = 12 cm, width = 7 cm. A semicircle with diameter = 7 cm replaces one width side.

Step 1: Rectangle perimeter = 2 × (12 + 7) = 2 × 19 = 38 cm
Step 2: Subtract the replaced width = 38 − 7 = 31 cm
Step 3: Semicircle arc length = π × 7 ÷ 2 = 3.14 × 7 ÷ 2 = 10.99 cm
Step 4: Composite perimeter = 31 + 10.99 = **41.99 cm**

---

## 7. Common Mistakes

1. **Forgetting to subtract the shared edge** when combining shapes.
2. **Using full circle circumference** instead of semicircle arc length.
3. **Including the diameter** of the semicircle when it's an internal edge.
4. **Arithmetic errors** in multi-step calculations — always verify each step.
