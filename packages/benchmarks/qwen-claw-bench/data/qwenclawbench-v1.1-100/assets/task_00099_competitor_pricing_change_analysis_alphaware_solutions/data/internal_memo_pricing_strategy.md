# Internal Memo: Competitive Pricing Strategy Assessment

**From:** Michael Torres, VP of Product Strategy  
**To:** Executive Leadership Team  
**Date:** April 12, 2024  
**Subject:** AlphaWare Solutions — Pricing & Feature Gap Analysis  
**Classification:** Internal — Confidential  

---

## Summary

Following AlphaWare Solutions' recent Enterprise plan price increase (from $279/mo to $299/mo in March 2024), I wanted to provide an updated assessment of our competitive positioning against them. The bottom line: **we maintain a strong competitive advantage on both price and AI capabilities.**

## AlphaWare's Current Pricing (as of April 2024)

| Plan | Price | Seats | AI Features |
|------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Starter | $29/mo | 5 | None |
| Professional | $129/mo | 20 | None |
| Enterprise | $299/mo | 50 | None |

## Our Pricing

| Plan | Price | Seats | AI Features |
|------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Starter | $49/mo | 5 | No |
| Professional | $149/mo | 25 | Add-on available |
| Enterprise | $279/mo | 75 | **Included** |

## Key Observations

### 1. Price Advantage at Enterprise Tier

At the Enterprise level, we are now **$20/mo cheaper** than AlphaWare ($279 vs. $299) while offering **50% more seats** (75 vs. 50). Their March price increase widened this gap in our favor. This is a significant talking point for our sales team.

### 2. AI Feature Gap is Widening

After extensive analysis of AlphaWare's product roadmap signals — including their public blog, job postings, patent filings, and conference presentations — **we see no indication that AlphaWare has AI features on their roadmap for 2024.** Their engineering job postings are focused on infrastructure and integrations, with zero ML/AI-related roles listed.

Their CEO's keynote at SaaSConnect 2024 (February) made no mention of AI capabilities. Their Q1 product update focused entirely on new integrations and UI improvements.

**Assessment: AlphaWare is at least 12-18 months behind us on AI capabilities.** This gap is likely to widen as we continue investing in our AI suite.

### 3. Strategic Implications

- AlphaWare's decision to raise prices without adding AI features suggests they are either confident in their integration-focused value proposition or are under margin pressure.
- Their 50-seat Enterprise cap at $299/mo makes them vulnerable to competitors (like us) who offer more seats at a lower price.
- We should continue to emphasize our AI capabilities and seat advantage in competitive deals against AlphaWare.

## Recommendation

**No pricing changes are needed at this time.** Our Enterprise plan at $279/mo with 75 seats and included AI features is well-positioned against AlphaWare's $299/mo offering with 50 seats and no AI. I recommend we:

1. Update competitive battle cards to emphasize the $20/mo price advantage
2. Train sales team on AI differentiation messaging
3. Continue monitoring AlphaWare's product and pricing pages for changes
4. Revisit this assessment in Q3 2024

**We maintain a strong competitive advantage on both price and AI capabilities.** AlphaWare would need to make significant moves on both fronts to close the gap.
