Notes on Margin Trading (融资融券) - Internal Reference
=========================================================

Date: 2024-07-20
Author: Risk Management Team

1. MARGIN TRADING OVERVIEW
--------------------------
Margin trading allows investors to borrow funds (融资) to buy securities
or borrow securities (融券) to sell short. This is a leveraged trading
mechanism available only to qualified investors.

2. ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------
- Account must have been open for at least 6 months
- Average daily assets >= 500,000 CNY over the past 20 trading days
- Risk tolerance assessment: C4 or above
- No significant compliance violations in the past 2 years
- Must sign margin trading agreement

3. MARGIN RATIOS
-----------------
- Initial margin ratio: >= 100% (as of Oct 2023, reduced from 100%)
  * Actually the regulation allows brokers to set, typically 100%
- Maintenance margin ratio: >= 130% (warning line)
  * Below 130%: margin call issued
  * Below 110%: forced liquidation may occur
- Collateral conversion rates:
  * Cash: 100%
  * Government bonds: 95%
  * ETFs: 80-90%
  * A-share stocks: 50-70% (varies by stock)
  * ST stocks: 0% (not accepted as collateral)

4. INTEREST RATES
------------------
- Margin lending (融资) rate: ~5.8% - 8.0% annualized
- Securities lending (融券) rate: ~8.0% - 10.6% annualized
- Rates vary by broker and client tier
- Interest calculated daily, settled monthly

5. ELIGIBLE SECURITIES
-----------------------
- Not all stocks are eligible for margin trading
- Exchange publishes eligible list periodically
- Typically large-cap, liquid stocks
- ST stocks are NOT eligible
- New listings excluded for initial period

6. RISK WARNINGS
-----------------
- Leverage amplifies both gains and losses
- Forced liquidation risk during market downturns
- Interest costs reduce net returns
- Margin calls may require immediate action
- Short squeeze risk for securities lending

7. RELEVANCE TO PAPER TRADING
-------------------------------
*** IMPORTANT: Margin trading features should NOT be included in
standard paper trading simulations. Paper trading should model
cash-only accounts with standard T+1 settlement rules. ***

Margin trading adds significant complexity:
- Different fee structures
- Interest calculations
- Margin maintenance requirements
- Short selling mechanics

For paper trading purposes, focus on:
- Cash account mechanics
- Standard commission/tax/fee structure
- T+1 settlement
- Long-only positions
