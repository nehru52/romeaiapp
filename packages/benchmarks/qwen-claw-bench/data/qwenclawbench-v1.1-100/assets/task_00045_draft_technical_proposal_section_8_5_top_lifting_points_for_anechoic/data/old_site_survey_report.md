## 1. Executive Summary

A preliminary site survey was conducted on June 12–14, 2021 to assess the existing building structure and determine feasibility for installation of top lifting points within the proposed anechoic chamber. This report documents the findings and provides initial recommendations for the lifting point layout.

> **Note:** This report is based on the Phase I conceptual design. Final design parameters are subject to change during detailed design.

---

## 2. Building Assessment

### 2.1 Structural System
The existing building is a portal frame structure with a clear span of 18 meters and eave height of 17 meters. The roof structure consists of welded steel trusses at 6-meter centers.

### 2.2 Available Clear Height
Based on measurements taken at 12 locations across the roof area:
- Minimum clear height from finished floor to underside of roof truss bottom chord: **15.0 m**
- Maximum clear height: **15.4 m**
- Average clear height: **15.2 m**

> The available clear height of **15 m** is considered adequate for the preliminary chamber design, which requires a minimum working height of 14 m for the antenna positioner and source antenna.

### 2.3 Roof Truss Condition
The roof trusses were found to be in good structural condition. Minor surface corrosion was observed on bottom chord members in the northeast corner, likely due to a previous roof leak (since repaired). No structural deficiency was identified.

---

## 3. Lifting Point Recommendations

### 3.1 Grid Layout
Based on the preliminary chamber dimensions (18m × 12m internal) and anticipated equipment weights, the following lifting point grid is recommended:

- **Grid spacing:** 2.5 m × 2.5 m (longitudinal × transverse)
- **Coverage area:** Full ceiling area of the anechoic chamber
- **Number of standard points:** Approximately 35

### 3.2 Embed Plate Specification
For the preliminary design, the following embed plate is recommended:

- **Plate size:** 200 mm × 200 mm × 16 mm
- **Material:** Q235B
- **Anchor bolts:** 4 × M12, Grade 4.6, embedment depth 120 mm
- **Surface treatment:** Zinc-rich primer + topcoat

### 3.3 Capacity Rating
- **Single point safe working load:** 0.5 t (4.9 kN)
- **Safety factor:** 3.0 (against ultimate capacity)
- **Design basis:** Maximum anticipated suspended load of 0.3 t per point (RF absorber panels + lighting fixtures)

### 3.4 Hoist Track Provision
A single overhead hoist track is recommended along the chamber centerline for equipment installation and maintenance. Specific hoist track lifting point requirements will be determined during detailed design.

---

## 4. Constraints and Limitations

1. The available clear height of 15 m limits the maximum chamber internal height to approximately 14 m after accounting for absorber tip clearance and structural depth.
2. Several existing roof penetrations (HVAC, skylights) will need to be relocated or sealed.
3. The northeast corner truss members require corrosion remediation before lifting points are installed.
4. Column grid spacing of 6 m constrains the maximum hoist track span.

---

## 5. Recommendations for Next Phase

1. Proceed with detailed structural analysis of the roof trusses to confirm lifting point capacity.
2. Engage a specialist shielding contractor to confirm EMC requirements for lifting point penetrations.
3. Update the lifting point specification once the final chamber dimensions are confirmed.
4. Consider upgrading the embed plate size if the equipment weight estimates increase during detailed design.
