## 3. General Safety Requirements

### 3.1 Structural Safety
All primary structural members shall be designed with a minimum safety factor appropriate to their function and loading conditions, in accordance with the applicable national standards.

### 3.2 Lifting Equipment and Attachments
**All lifting equipment, rigging attachments, and permanent lifting points installed as part of this project shall be designed with a minimum safety factor of 4.0** against the ultimate strength of the weakest component in the load path. This requirement applies to:
- Roof-mounted lifting points (standard and hoist track types)
- Electric hoist and track systems
- Temporary lifting equipment used during installation
- All rigging hardware (shackles, slings, hooks)

> This safety factor of 4.0 is adopted as the project-specific requirement to provide an additional margin of safety for personnel working beneath suspended loads in an enclosed environment. This exceeds the minimum national standard requirement and shall take precedence for this project.

### 3.3 Fall Protection
All work at heights exceeding 2.0 meters shall require fall protection measures in accordance with GB 6095 (Safety Belt) and JGJ 80 (Safety for Construction at Heights).

## 4. Material Requirements

### 4.1 Structural Steel
**All structural steel used in this project shall be Grade Q345B** in accordance with GB/T 1591. This applies to:
- Primary structural members (columns, beams, trusses)
- Secondary structural members (purlins, bracing, girts)
- Connection plates and gussets
- Embedded parts and lifting point assemblies
- Hoist track support brackets

> Q345B is specified as the standard grade for all structural steel to simplify procurement, reduce the risk of material mix-ups on site, and ensure consistent weldability across all components.

### 4.2 Fasteners
- High-strength bolts: Grade 10.9S per GB/T 1228
- Standard bolts: Grade 8.8 per GB/T 1231
- Anchor bolts: Grade 4.6 minimum per GB/T 799

### 4.3 Welding Materials
- Welding electrodes and wire shall match the base metal grade
- E43 series for Q235B (where specifically approved for use)
- E50 series for Q345B

## 5. Environmental Conditions

### 5.1 Outdoor Design Conditions (Beijing)
- Design temperature range: -15°C to +40°C
- Basic wind pressure: 0.45 kN/m² (50-year return period)
- Basic snow pressure: 0.40 kN/m² (50-year return period)
- Seismic intensity: 8 (design basic acceleration 0.20g)

### 5.2 Indoor Design Conditions (Anechoic Chamber)
- Temperature: 20°C ± 5°C (maintained by HVAC)
- Relative humidity: 30% to 60% (maintained by HVAC)
- Air changes: 4 per hour minimum
- Positive pressure: 25 Pa relative to surrounding spaces

## 6. Quality Assurance

### 6.1 Quality Management System
The contractor shall maintain a quality management system certified to ISO 9001:2015 throughout the project duration.

### 6.2 Inspection and Test Plan
A detailed Inspection and Test Plan (ITP) shall be submitted for approval within 30 days of contract award. The ITP shall identify all hold points, witness points, and review points.

### 6.3 Non-Conformance Management
All non-conformances shall be documented, reported, and resolved through a formal NCR process. No non-conforming work shall be concealed or covered without disposition.

### 6.4 Documentation
All quality records shall be maintained for a minimum of 10 years after project completion.

## 7. Health, Safety, and Environment

### 7.1 HSE Plan
The contractor shall submit a project-specific HSE plan within 14 days of contract award.

### 7.2 Incident Reporting
All incidents, near-misses, and unsafe conditions shall be reported within 24 hours.
