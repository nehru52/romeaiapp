# RF Absorber Installation Manual
## Pyramid-Type Microwave Absorbers for Anechoic Chamber Lining
### Manufacturer: Siepel / Emerson & Cuming
### Document: ABS-INST-2024-Rev03

---

## 1. Product Description

### 1.1 Absorber Type
- **Model:** APM-24 Pyramid Absorber
- **Pyramid height:** 610 mm (24 inches)
- **Base dimensions:** 610 mm × 610 mm
- **Overall thickness (pyramid + base):** 660 mm
- **Weight per panel:** 2.5 kg (typical)
- **Material:** Polyurethane foam, carbon-loaded
- **Color:** Dark grey / black

### 1.2 RF Performance
- Reflectivity at normal incidence:
  - 200 MHz: -20 dB
  - 500 MHz: -35 dB
  - 1 GHz: -45 dB
  - 10 GHz: -50 dB
  - 18 GHz: -50 dB

### 1.3 Fire Rating
- **Classification:** UL 94 HF-1 (self-extinguishing)
- **Flame spread index:** ≤ 25 (ASTM E84)
- **Smoke developed index:** ≤ 50 (ASTM E84)
- Fire-retardant treatment is factory-applied and integral to the foam formulation.

---

## 2. Storage and Handling

- Store absorbers in a dry, clean environment at 10–35°C.
- Keep panels in original packaging until ready for installation.
- Do not stack pallets more than 3 high.
- Avoid exposure to direct sunlight, which may degrade the foam over time.
- Handle with clean, dry gloves. Oils and contaminants may affect adhesive bonding.

---

## 3. Surface Preparation

### 3.1 Substrate Requirements
- The mounting surface (shielding panel) must be clean, dry, and free of:
  - Loose rust or mill scale
  - Oil, grease, or cutting fluid residue
  - Dust, concrete splatter, or debris
  - Non-conductive paint (unless approved by adhesive manufacturer)

### 3.2 Cleaning Procedure
1. Remove loose debris with compressed air or vacuum.
2. Wipe surface with solvent (MEK or isopropyl alcohol) using clean lint-free cloths.
3. Allow surface to dry completely (minimum 15 minutes).
4. Verify surface cleanliness with water break test if required.

---

## 4. Adhesive Specification

### 4.1 Recommended Adhesives
| Adhesive Type | Product Example | Application Method | Cure Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contact cement | 3M 78 Spray | Spray both surfaces | 30 min tack, 24 hr full |
| Epoxy paste | Hysol EA 9394 | Trowel / notched spreader | 24 hr at 25°C |
| Pressure-sensitive tape | 3M VHB 4941 | Peel and stick | Immediate, 72 hr full |

### 4.2 Adhesive Coverage
- Contact cement: 200–250 g/m² per surface
- Epoxy paste: 3–5 mm bead on 150 mm grid pattern
- VHB tape: 4 strips per panel (minimum 25 mm wide)

## 5. Installation Sequence

### 5.1 General Approach
Installation shall proceed in the following order:
1. **Ceiling** — Install ceiling absorbers first
2. **Upper walls** — Work from top down
3. **Lower walls** — Complete wall coverage
4. **Floor** (if applicable) — Install walk-on absorber tiles last

### 5.2 Ceiling Installation

> **Important:** Ceiling absorber installation requires access to the full ceiling area at working height. Temporary scaffolding, mobile elevated work platforms (MEWPs), or suspended work platforms shall be used. **The ceiling lifting points are designed for equipment and maintenance loads — they are NOT rated for supporting scaffolding or personnel platforms.** All temporary access equipment must be independently supported from the floor.

#### Procedure:
1. Erect scaffolding or position MEWP to provide access within arm's reach of the ceiling surface.
2. Apply adhesive to the ceiling shielding panel surface per Section 4.
3. Apply adhesive to the absorber panel base.
4. Wait for appropriate tack time (contact cement) or proceed immediately (epoxy/tape).
5. Press absorber panel firmly against ceiling surface, ensuring full contact across the base.
6. Hold or brace panel in position for minimum 60 seconds.
7. For contact cement, no additional bracing is needed after initial tack.
8. For epoxy, temporary bracing (foam wedges or spring clips) may be needed for 2–4 hours until initial cure.

### 5.3 Wall Installation
1. Start at the top of the wall and work downward.
2. Apply adhesive and mount panels in horizontal rows.
3. Stagger vertical joints between rows (brick pattern) for improved RF performance.
4. Trim edge panels as needed using a hot-wire cutter or serrated knife.

---

## 6. Ambient Conditions During Installation

- **Temperature:** 15°C to 30°C (optimal for adhesive performance)
- **Relative humidity:** 30% to 70%
- **Ventilation:** Ensure adequate ventilation when using solvent-based adhesives. Use respiratory protection if required.
- **Lighting:** Provide temporary lighting sufficient for visual inspection of adhesive coverage and panel alignment.

---

## 7. Quality Inspection

### 7.1 Visual Inspection
- Check all panels for full base contact (no gaps or bridging).
- Verify panel alignment and pattern continuity.
- Inspect for adhesive squeeze-out or contamination on pyramid surfaces.

### 7.2 Tap Test
- Lightly tap each panel to verify adhesion. A hollow sound indicates incomplete bonding.
- Re-bond or replace any panels that fail the tap test.

### 7.3 Documentation
- Record installation date, ambient conditions, adhesive batch numbers.
- Photograph completed surfaces for as-built records.

---

## 8. Safety Precautions

- Wear cut-resistant gloves when handling absorber panels (edges may be sharp after cutting).
- Use respiratory protection when cutting foam (dust) or applying solvent-based adhesives.
- Follow MSDS/SDS requirements for all adhesives and solvents.
- Ensure scaffolding and MEWPs are inspected and certified before use.
- No hot work (welding, grinding) shall be performed in the chamber after absorber installation begins.
