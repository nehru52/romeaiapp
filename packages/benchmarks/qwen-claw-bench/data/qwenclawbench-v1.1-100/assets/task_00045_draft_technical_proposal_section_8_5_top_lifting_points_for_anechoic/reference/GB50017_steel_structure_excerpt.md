# GB 50017-2017 — Standard for Design of Steel Structures
## Selected Excerpts Relevant to Embedded Parts and Lifting Point Design

> **Disclaimer:** This document contains selected excerpts for project reference only. For authoritative requirements, refer to the full published standard.

### Clause 4.3 — Material Selection

**4.3.1** Structural steel used for load-bearing members shall comply with the requirements of GB/T 700 (for Q235 grades) or GB/T 1591 (for Q345 and higher grades).

**4.3.2** For Q235B steel:
- Minimum yield strength: 235 MPa (thickness ≤ 16mm), 225 MPa (16mm < thickness ≤ 40mm)
- Minimum tensile strength: 370–500 MPa
- Minimum elongation: 26%
- Impact toughness at 20°C: ≥ 27 J

**4.3.3** For Q345B steel:
- Minimum yield strength: 345 MPa (thickness ≤ 16mm), 325 MPa (16mm < thickness ≤ 35mm)
- Minimum tensile strength: 470–630 MPa
- Minimum elongation: 22%
- Impact toughness at 20°C: ≥ 34 J

**4.3.5** Material certificates (mill test reports) shall be provided for all structural steel. Material shall be traceable to heat number.

### Clause 8.5 — Anchor Bolt and Embedded Part Design

**8.5.1** Embedded plates subjected to tension and shear loads shall be designed considering:
- Tensile capacity of anchor bolts
- Bearing capacity of the concrete surrounding the anchor
- Shear capacity at the steel-concrete interface
- Combined tension-shear interaction

**8.5.2** The design bearing capacity of an embedded plate under tension shall be calculated as:

> N_d = n × A_s × f_t / γ_R

Where:
- N_d = design tensile capacity (kN)
- n = number of anchor bolts
- A_s = tensile stress area of anchor bolt (mm²)
- f_t = design tensile strength of anchor bolt material (MPa)
- γ_R = resistance partial factor (≥ 1.25 for normal conditions)

**8.5.3** The minimum embedment depth of anchor bolts shall not be less than 8 times the bolt diameter for straight anchors, or 4 times the bolt diameter for hooked or headed anchors.

**8.5.4** Edge distance of anchor bolts from the concrete face shall not be less than 5 times the bolt diameter.

**8.5.5** Spacing between adjacent anchor bolts shall not be less than 6 times the bolt diameter.

### Clause 9.2 — Weld Strength Requirements

**9.2.1** Fillet welds shall be designed based on the effective throat area and the design shear strength of the weld metal.

**9.2.2** Design strength values for manual arc welding with E43 electrodes (matching Q235B):
- Fillet weld shear strength: f_w^f = 160 MPa
- Butt weld tension/compression: f_w^t = 185 MPa

**9.2.3** Design strength values for manual arc welding with E50 electrodes (matching Q345B):
- Fillet weld shear strength: f_w^f = 200 MPa
- Butt weld tension/compression: f_w^t = 215 MPa

**9.2.4** All welds on lifting-related components shall be full-penetration butt welds or load-rated fillet welds. Intermittent welds are not permitted for lifting attachments.

**9.2.5** Weld quality for lifting attachments shall be Grade I (first class) per GB 50205, requiring 100% ultrasonic or radiographic inspection.

### Clause 11.4 — Safety Factors for Lifting and Rigging Attachments

**11.4.1** Permanent lifting points and rigging attachments built into the structure shall be designed with a minimum safety factor of **3.0** against the ultimate tensile strength of the weakest component in the load path.

**11.4.2** The safety factor shall be calculated as:

> SF = Ultimate Capacity / (Working Load Limit × Dynamic Amplification Factor)

Where the dynamic amplification factor shall not be less than 1.2 for electric hoists.

**11.4.3** For lifting points subject to multi-directional loading or eccentric loads, the safety factor shall be increased to not less than 4.0.

**11.4.4** Proof load testing shall be conducted at 1.25 times the rated working load. The test load shall be held for not less than 10 minutes. No permanent deformation or cracking shall be observed.

### Clause 15.3 — Anti-Corrosion Requirements

**15.3.1** Steel structures in industrial environments with moderate to high humidity shall be protected against corrosion by one of the following methods:
- Hot-dip galvanizing per GB/T 13912 (minimum coating thickness 85 μm)
- Multi-coat paint system (primer + intermediate + topcoat, minimum total DFT 200 μm)
- Thermal spray zinc/aluminum coating

**15.3.2** For enclosed environments with controlled humidity (RH < 60%), the minimum coating system may be reduced, subject to approval by the design engineer.

**15.3.3** All cut edges, bolt holes, and weld areas shall receive touch-up treatment matching the original coating system.

### Clause 16.7 — Inspection and Acceptance of Embedded Parts

**16.7.1** Embedded parts shall be inspected prior to concrete placement for:
- Correct position (tolerance: ±10 mm in plan, ±5 mm in elevation)
- Correct orientation and levelness (tolerance: ±5 mm over plate width)
- Anchor bolt projection length
- Weld quality (visual inspection 100%, NDT per design requirements)
- Secure temporary fixing to formwork

**16.7.2** After concrete placement and curing, exposed surfaces of embedded plates shall be inspected for:
- Plate flatness (tolerance: ±2 mm)
- Surface cleanliness (free from concrete splatter)
- Coating integrity

**16.7.3** Documentation required for acceptance:
- Material certificates for plate and anchor bolts
- Welder qualification certificates
- NDT reports
- Dimensional survey report
- Load test report (where required)
- Photographic records
