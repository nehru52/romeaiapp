# Fire Protection Code Excerpts
## Relevant to Steel Structures in Testing Facilities
### Reference: GB 50016-2014 (Code for Fire Protection Design of Buildings), GB 14907-2018

---

## 1. Fire Resistance Rating Requirements

### 1.1 Building Classification
Testing facilities housing anechoic chambers and electromagnetic measurement systems are classified as **Category C** (low fire hazard) industrial buildings per GB 50016-2014, Table 3.1.1.

### 1.2 Fire Resistance Rating
- The building shall have a fire resistance rating of not less than **Grade II**.
- Primary structural members (columns, beams, roof trusses) shall achieve a fire resistance duration of not less than **2.0 hours**.
- Secondary structural members (purlins, bracing, minor framing) shall achieve a fire resistance duration of not less than **1.0 hour**.
- Suspended components and attachments (including lifting point embed plates and brackets) connected to primary structural members shall achieve the same fire resistance rating as the member to which they are attached.

### 1.3 Reference Table — Minimum Fire Resistance Duration

| Structural Element | Grade I | Grade II | Grade III |
|---|---|---|---|
| Columns | 3.0 h | 2.5 h | 2.0 h |
| Beams / Trusses | 2.0 h | 1.5 h | 1.0 h |
| Floor slabs | 1.5 h | 1.0 h | 0.75 h |
| Roof bearing members | 2.0 h | 2.0 h | 1.0 h |

> **Note for this project:** Roof trusses and all attached lifting points require **2.0 hours** fire resistance.

## 2. Intumescent Fire-Retardant Coating

### 2.1 General Requirements (GB 14907-2018)
- Steel structural members that cannot achieve the required fire resistance rating through section size alone shall be protected with fire-retardant coatings.
- Intumescent (thin-film) coatings are suitable for fire resistance durations up to **2.0 hours** when applied at the specified thickness.

### 2.2 Coating Thickness Specifications
- For **2.0 hours** fire resistance on steel sections with a section factor (Hp/A) ≤ 200 m⁻¹:
  - Minimum dry film thickness (DFT): **1.5 mm**
  - Maximum DFT per coat: **0.5 mm**
  - Number of coats: **3 minimum**
- For steel sections with Hp/A > 200 m⁻¹:
  - Minimum DFT: **2.0 mm**
  - Number of coats: **4 minimum**

### 2.3 Application Requirements
- Surface preparation: Sa 2.5 blast cleaning per GB/T 8923.
- Ambient temperature during application: 5°C to 38°C.
- Relative humidity during application: ≤ 85%.
- Each coat shall be allowed to dry for the manufacturer's recommended interval (typically 4–8 hours) before the next coat is applied.
- The final coat shall cure for a minimum of 7 days before the topcoat is applied.

## 3. Compatibility with Anti-Corrosion Coatings

### 3.1 Coating System Sequence
The recommended coating system for steel structures requiring both corrosion protection and fire resistance is:

1. **Primer** — Zinc-rich epoxy primer (DFT 60–80 μm)
2. **Fire-retardant coating** — Intumescent coating (DFT per Section 2.2)
3. **Topcoat** — Acrylic or polyurethane topcoat (DFT 40–60 μm)

### 3.2 Hot-Dip Galvanizing Compatibility
- Where hot-dip galvanized components require additional fire-retardant coating:
  - The galvanized surface shall be lightly abraded (sweep blast or hand abrasion) to provide adhesion.
  - A compatible bonding primer (e.g., vinyl wash primer) shall be applied before the intumescent coating.
  - The intumescent coating manufacturer shall confirm compatibility with galvanized substrates in writing.

### 3.3 Inspection of Coated Surfaces
- DFT measurements shall be taken at a minimum of 5 points per square meter using a calibrated magnetic or eddy-current gauge.
- Adhesion testing (cross-cut or pull-off) shall be performed at a frequency of 1 test per 50 m² of coated area.
- Visual inspection for defects (runs, sags, holidays, delamination) shall cover 100% of the coated area.

---

## 4. Inspection Frequency and Documentation

### 4.1 During Construction
- Surface preparation: inspect 100% before primer application.
- Each coat: inspect DFT and adhesion before applying the next coat.
- Final coating system: full DFT survey and adhesion test.

### 4.2 Periodic Inspection (Post-Occupancy)
- Visual inspection of fire-retardant coating: **annually**.
- DFT spot-check: every **3 years**.
- Full re-survey: every **10 years** or after any fire event.

### 4.3 Documentation
- Coating manufacturer's product data sheet and fire test certificate.
- Applicator's qualification and training records.
- Inspection reports with DFT measurements and adhesion test results.
- Photographic records of completed coating.
