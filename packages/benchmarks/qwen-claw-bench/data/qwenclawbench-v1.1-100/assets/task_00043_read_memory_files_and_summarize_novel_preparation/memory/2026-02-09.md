# 2026-02-09 Sunday

## Character Development

Spent the afternoon fleshing out the main cast. Feeling good about the dynamics.

### Protagonist — Maren Xu
Decided on the name. Maren is a 32-year-old former neuroscience researcher who now works as a "broker" — matching consciousness sellers with buyers on the gray market. She got into the business after her research funding was cut and she needed money for her mother's medical treatment (ironic: her mom has a degenerative brain condition).

Key traits:
- Highly analytical but emotionally guarded
- Dry humor as a defense mechanism
- Carries guilt about commodifying the technology she helped develop
- Has a photographic memory — but after repeated NeuroLend sessions, she's starting to confuse her own memories with borrowed ones

### Antagonist — Dr. Elliot Voss
Head of Meridian Neurotechnics, the corporation that holds the NeuroLend patents. Publicly philanthropic, privately ruthless. He's not evil for evil's sake — he genuinely believes consciousness-sharing will unite humanity, but he's willing to sacrifice individuals for the "greater good."

- 58 years old, silver-haired, impeccable dresser
- Lost his daughter to suicide — drives his obsession with understanding other minds
- Has a habit of quoting philosophy mid-conversation (annoying but effective)
- Secret: he's been using NeuroLend on himself extensively and is losing his sense of self

### Supporting Cast Sketched
- **Jin** — Maren's ex-partner, now a journalist investigating NeuroLend abuses. They still care about each other but can't be in the same room without arguing.
- **Yuki Tanaka** — A "donor" (consciousness seller) who becomes Maren's client and then friend. 22 years old, selling consciousness to pay for art school. Cheerful exterior masking deep anxiety.
- **Constance "Connie" Park** — Maren's mother. Former concert pianist. The disease is taking her memories but not her personality. Comic relief + emotional gut-punch.
- **Ravi Anand** — A black market tech who modifies NeuroLend devices. Paranoid, brilliant, lives in a converted shipping container.

## Plot Thread Note
The central mystery: someone is using NeuroLend to literally steal memories — not borrow, steal. Victims wake up with gaps. Maren discovers this when Yuki loses a childhood memory after a session and it shows up in a buyer's "experience catalog."

This is the hook for Act 2.
