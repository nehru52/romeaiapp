# 2026-02-07 Friday

## World-Building Notes

Started building out the world for the consciousness-lending story.

### Technology — NeuroLend System
- **Device**: A headband-like neural interface called a "bridge." Two people wear connected bridges. One is the "donor" (shares consciousness), the other is the "receiver."
- **Duration**: Sessions typically last 1-4 hours. Beyond 6 hours, there's risk of "bleed" — personality traits or memories leaking between participants.
- **Side effects**: Mild disorientation after sessions, occasional phantom emotions (feeling the donor's feelings hours later), and in rare cases, identity confusion.
- **Legal status**: Approved for medical/therapeutic use only. Recreational and commercial use is technically illegal but widely practiced.
- **Black market modifications**: Unlicensed technicians can modify bridges to extend sessions, amplify emotional intensity, or (rumored) record and replay consciousness without a live donor.

### Setting — Neo-Portland (Portland, Oregon, 2041)
- Chose Portland because: tech culture + counterculture + rainy atmosphere
- The city has become a hub for NeuroLend gray market activity
- The **Pearl District** has been rebranded as "The Mesh" — full of consciousness bars and experience parlors
- The **East Side** is where the underground brokers and modified-bridge dealers operate
- **Meridian Neurotechnics** HQ is a gleaming campus on the waterfront, visible from everywhere — a constant reminder of corporate power

### Social Dynamics
- "Donors" are often from lower economic classes — selling consciousness is the new gig economy
- "Receivers" are wealthy individuals, thrill-seekers, or people seeking therapeutic experiences
- There's a growing political movement called **"Own Your Mind"** pushing for stricter regulation
- Counter-movement: **"Open Consciousness Coalition"** arguing all experience should be shared freely
- Religious groups are split — some see it as blasphemous (only God should share in human experience), others as transcendent

### Key Locations
1. **The Threshold** — Maren's brokerage office, disguised as a meditation center in an old converted church
2. **Meridian Neurotechnics HQ** — Corporate campus, sterile and imposing
3. **Ravi's Container** — Shipping container workshop in an industrial lot, walls lined with modified bridges
4. **Connie's apartment** — Maren's mother's place, filled with sheet music and photographs, slowly being emptied as Connie forgets what things mean
5. **The Float** — An underground consciousness bar where you can sample 15-minute "tastes" of other people's experiences
