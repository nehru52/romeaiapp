### Neuroscience Notes
- Mirror neurons: cells that fire both when we act and when we observe the same action in others. Basis for empathy.
- Default mode network: brain regions active during daydreaming and self-referential thought. Could be the "channel" that NeuroLend taps into.
- Dissociative identity — what happens when one brain hosts multiple consciousness patterns?
- Neuroplasticity: the brain physically changes based on experience. If you borrow someone's consciousness, does your brain literally reshape?

### Philosophical Framework
- Personal identity: Derek Parfit's "Reasons and Persons" — are we the same person over time?
- Ship of Theseus — if you gradually replace your experiences with someone else's, are you still you?
- Nagel's "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?" — the hard problem of consciousness, subjective experience
- Buddhist concept of anatta (no-self) — maybe there was never a fixed "you" to lose
