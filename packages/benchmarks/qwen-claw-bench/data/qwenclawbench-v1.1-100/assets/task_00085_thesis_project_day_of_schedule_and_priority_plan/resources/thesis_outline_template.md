# Standard Thesis Outline Template

This template provides the required structure for all undergraduate and graduate theses submitted to the Department of Computer Science. Follow this outline to ensure your thesis meets all formatting and content requirements.

---

## Abstract
- **Description:** A concise summary of the entire thesis, including the research problem, methodology, key findings, and conclusions.
- **Approximate Length:** 1 page (250–350 words)
- **Tips:** Write this last, after all other sections are complete. Avoid citations in the abstract.

---

## Table of Contents
- **Description:** Auto-generated list of all chapters, sections, and sub-sections with page numbers.
- **Approximate Length:** 1–2 pages
- **Tips:** Use your word processor's built-in heading styles to auto-generate this section.

---

## Chapter 1: Introduction
- **Description:** Introduces the research topic, states the problem, defines the scope, and outlines the objectives of the study. Should include the research questions or hypotheses.
- **Approximate Length:** 5–8 pages
- **Key Elements:**
  - Background and context
  - Problem statement
  - Research objectives
  - Significance of the study
  - Thesis organization overview

---

## Chapter 2: Literature Review
- **Description:** A comprehensive review of existing research and theoretical frameworks relevant to the topic. Identifies gaps in current knowledge that the thesis aims to address.
- **Approximate Length:** 10–15 pages
- **Key Elements:**
  - Thematic or chronological organization of prior work
  - Critical analysis of existing studies
  - Identification of research gaps
  - Theoretical framework

---

## Chapter 3: Methodology
- **Description:** Detailed description of the research design, data collection methods, tools, and analysis techniques used in the study.
- **Approximate Length:** 8–12 pages
- **Key Elements:**
  - Research design (qualitative, quantitative, mixed)
  - Population and sampling
  - Data collection instruments
  - Data analysis procedures
  - Ethical considerations
  - Validity and reliability

---

## Chapter 4: Results and Discussion
- **Description:** Presentation of findings with supporting data (tables, figures, charts) followed by interpretation and discussion in the context of existing literature.
- **Approximate Length:** 12–18 pages
- **Key Elements:**
  - Data presentation (organized by research question)
  - Statistical analysis results (if applicable)
  - Interpretation of findings
  - Comparison with prior studies
  - Unexpected results and explanations

---

## Chapter 5: Conclusion
- **Description:** Summary of key findings, contributions to the field, limitations of the study, and recommendations for future research.
- **Approximate Length:** 3–5 pages
- **Key Elements:**
  - Summary of main findings
  - Theoretical and practical implications
  - Limitations
  - Recommendations for future work
  - Concluding remarks

---

## References
- **Description:** Complete list of all sources cited in the thesis, formatted according to APA 7th Edition guidelines.
- **Approximate Length:** 3–6 pages
- **Tips:** Use a reference manager (e.g., Zotero, Mendeley) to ensure consistency. Minimum 30 references recommended for graduate theses.

---

## Appendices
- **Description:** Supplementary materials that support the thesis but are too detailed for the main text.
- **Approximate Length:** Variable
- **May Include:**
  - Survey instruments or questionnaires
  - Interview transcripts
  - Raw data tables
  - Code listings
  - Consent forms
  - Additional figures or charts
