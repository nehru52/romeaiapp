# Thesis Defense PPT — Structure and Content Guide

## Overview

This guide outlines the recommended structure, content requirements, and formatting tips for your thesis defense presentation. Follow these guidelines to ensure a professional and well-organized defense.

---

## PPT Structure

Your presentation should follow this sequence of sections:

1. **Title Slide**
   - Thesis title
   - Your full name and student ID
   - Department and university name
   - Advisor name
   - Date of defense

2. **Outline / Agenda**
   - Brief overview of the presentation structure
   - Helps the committee follow along

3. **Research Background**
   - Context and motivation for the research
   - Problem statement
   - Research questions or hypotheses

4. **Literature Review Summary**
   - Key prior work and theoretical framework
   - Identify the research gap your work addresses

5. **Methodology**
   - Research design and approach
   - Data collection methods
   - Analysis techniques
   - Tools and software used

6. **Results**
   - Key findings presented with charts, tables, or figures
   - Statistical significance where applicable
   - Clear labeling of all visual elements

7. **Discussion and Conclusion**
   - Interpretation of results
   - Contributions to the field
   - Limitations of the study
   - Recommendations for future research

8. **Q&A Slide**
   - Simple slide inviting questions
   - Optional: include your contact information

---

## Slide Count

- **Recommended:** 15–20 slides total
- Fewer than 12 slides may appear underprepared
- More than 25 slides risks running over time

---

## Time Allocation

Total presentation time: approximately 15–20 minutes

| Section              | Suggested Time |
|----------------------|----------------|
| Title & Outline      | 1–2 minutes    |
| Research Background   | 2–3 minutes    |
| Literature Review     | 2–3 minutes    |
| Methodology           | 3–4 minutes    |
| Results               | 3–4 minutes    |
| Discussion/Conclusion | 2–3 minutes    |
| Q&A                   | 5–10 minutes   |

---

## Required Elements

Every thesis defense PPT **must** include:

- [ ] University logo on the title slide
- [ ] Advisor's full name and title
- [ ] Date of the defense
- [ ] Student ID number
- [ ] Slide numbers on every slide (except title)
- [ ] Consistent font and color scheme throughout

---

## Formatting Tips

- **Font:** Use a clean sans-serif font (e.g., Arial, Calibri) at minimum 24pt for body text and 32pt for titles.
- **Colors:** Stick to 2–3 colors maximum. Use the university's official color palette if available.
- **Backgrounds:** Keep backgrounds simple — white or light gray works best.
- **Images:** Use high-resolution images only. Cite sources for any borrowed figures.
- **Animations:** Minimal or none. Avoid distracting transitions.
- **Bullet Points:** No more than 5–6 per slide. Use concise phrases, not full sentences.
- **Charts/Graphs:** Label all axes, include legends, and use consistent styling.

---

## Final Checklist

Before submitting your PPT:

1. Spell-check all slides
2. Verify all figures and tables are legible
3. Test the presentation on the projector/screen you'll use
4. Ensure all embedded media (videos, audio) works
5. Save a backup copy in PDF format
6. Practice your delivery at least twice
