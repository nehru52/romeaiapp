# E1X3D Risk Register

Risks specific to the **E1X3D** 3D-stacked wafer-mesh direction
(`docs/arch/e1x3d-wafer-stack.md`). The X/Y wafer mesh inherits the E1X risk
posture; this register covers the **Z tier-stack**, which is the novel,
lower-TRL, thermally-limited part. Severity/likelihood follow the program
`risk-register.md` convention. Every risk maps to a fail-closed gate or a
documented BLOCKED dependency — none is left as prose only.

| Risk | Owner | Status | Severity | Likelihood | Trigger | Failure mode | Mitigation | Evidence / Gate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stacked-logic thermal ceiling | thermal | Active | Critical | Very high | Any tier-count or power claim without a modeled per-tier power-density and peak-junction check. | Buried logic tiers superpose hotspots (~250 W/cm2 in stacked HP logic); the chip throttles or ages out. | Memory-on-logic (cool buffer tiers), <=4 logic tiers, <=~1.0 W/mm2 per tier, backside/dual-sided cooling, reduced V/f on stacked tiers; gate fails closed past the ceiling. | `thermal_model` in `compiler/runtime/e1x3d_wafer_model.py`; `eliza.e1x3d.thermal_model.v1` sidecar; `make e1x3d-benchmark` |
| Multi-tier yield compounding / W2W cannot sort | yield | Active | Critical | High | Any stacked-yield claim without per-tier Poisson + multiplicative bond-yield modeling. | Each added bonded tier multiplies in another `exp(-D*A)`; untested tall stacks collapse yield. | Die-to-wafer with KGD/KGS (never W2W) so only known-good tiers are bonded; fine-grained ~1.5% spares per tier + spare-plane harvesting; bond-yield gate. | `stack_yield_model`; `eliza.e1x3d.stack_yield_model.v1`; `make e1x3d-benchmark` |
| Monolithic-3D (MIV) TRL for fine per-PE folding | process | Active | Critical | High | Any "shrink the PE by slicing its logic across tiers" claim that assumes a production flow. | Fine per-PE logic folding needs sequential-integration (M3D) at ~70 nm MIV pitch, which is research-grade and thermally capped at 2-4 tiers. | Default to block-level SRAM-on-logic on production hybrid bonding; treat fine logic fold as an M3D-PDK-gated stretch; placement model reports the required bonding per split. | `e1x3d_placement_model.py` (`fine_logic_fold`); `make e1x3d-placement`; `research/threed_ic_2026/03_implementation/e1x3d_design_decisions.md` |
| No open 3D signoff path (commercial-only) | pd | Active | Critical | Very high | Any "3D taped out / signed off" claim from open tools. | 3D DRC/LVS, electrothermal, and SI/PI have no open-source signoff path; open flows are pseudo-3D prototypes only. | Prototype on Open3DBench / OpenROAD-Research (Pin-3D/Snap-3D) + HotSpot/3D-ICE; signoff requires Cadence Integrity 3D-IC / Synopsys 3DIC Compiler / Siemens Calibre 3D — recorded as BLOCKED. | `blocked_signoff_path` in `eliza.e1x3d.placement_feasibility.v1`; `make e1x3d-placement` |
| Dead-tier-aware Z routing is a research gap | architecture | Active | High | High | A "routes around any defect" claim that assumes whole-tier loss is handled like sparse faults. | Most 3D-NoC fault-tolerant routing assumes sparse faults, not a whole missing plane; a dead tier could strand a column. | Model bounded dead-tier-region repair + Z reroute now; require spare planes (`spare_tiers>=1`) and graceful column degradation for full-tier loss; full-tier-at-scale fails closed. | `DEAD_TIER_SCENARIO_3D`, `route`/`repair_map` in `e1x3d_wafer_model.py`; `make e1x3d-benchmark` (dead-tier Z paths) |
| Hybrid-bond pitch too coarse for fine split | package | Active | High | High | A per-PE tier-split claim on production ~6 um hybrid bonding. | Production hybrid bond (~6 um, ~28k vias/mm2) cannot carry a fine logic-fold's inter-tier via density. | Placement model checks via density vs a bonding catalog and reports the minimum pitch (block split fits ~6 um; fine fold needs ~1 um or MIV). | `evaluate_split` / `_feasible_bondings`; `make e1x3d-placement` |
| TSV/bond reliability + warpage in tall stacks | reliability | Monitoring | High | Medium | A multi-tier claim without CTE/thermal-cycling/EM/warpage budgeting. | CTE-mismatch stress, thermal-cycling fatigue, electromigration in small TSVs, and post-anneal warpage worsen with tier count. | Keep tier count low (2-3 active), TSV keep-out zones, thermal vias, KGD; treat reliability as a hardware-test BLOCKED dependency. | `docs/risks/e1x3d-risks.md`; `research/threed_ic_2026/02_analysis/3d_placement_benchmarks_yield_thermal.md` |
| DfT for 3D / KGD test cost | dft | Active | Medium | High | A test-coverage claim without per-tier wrapper + pre/post-bond test. | Stacked dies are untestable post-bond without IEEE 1838 DWR/SCM; MIV BIST is unsolved for M3D. | IEEE 1838 die wrapper + serial control, pre-bond KGD + post-bond KGS, per-tier MBIST; recorded as the test strategy in the yield model. | `stack_yield_model` `test_strategy`; design decisions doc |
| Row/column sparing has poor locality at wafer scale | architecture | Monitoring | Medium | High | A "repair is free" claim ignoring repaired-route hop length. | Row/col sparing remaps a defective core to a distant border band, creating long repaired neighbor routes (hundreds of hops at scale). | Document the limitation; the production answer is Cerebras-style fine-grained local sparing and/or spare planes; the model reports max/avg extra hops honestly. | `validate_repaired_mesh` (`max_repaired_neighbor_hops`); scaled report metrics |
| Overclaiming density (folding != 2x/tier) | program | Active | Medium | Medium | A "Nx density per tier" claim. | Two-tier folding buys ~40-64% footprint shrink (SRAM-tier limited), not a clean 2x/tier; overclaim erodes credibility. | Model footprint shrink from real area split (Open3DBench-grounded), report packing density honestly. | `core_xy_area_mm2` / `packing_density_ratio`; `eliza.e1x3d.placement_feasibility.v1` |
| Conflating planar Cerebras proof with Z novelty | program | Active | Medium | Medium | A claim that "wafer-scale 3D is proven" because Cerebras works. | Cerebras WSE is planar (2D) wafer-scale, not Z-stacked; citing it as proof of the Z-stack overstates maturity. | Keep X/Y (proven) and Z (novel) concerns separate in docs and gates; claim boundary states architecture-simulation only. | `docs/arch/e1x3d-wafer-stack.md` claim boundary; `00_index.md` headline conclusions |

## Non-goals (E1X3D v0)

- No real 3D tapeout, 3D DRC/LVS, or electrothermal/SI-PI signoff (commercial-only).
- No measured silicon, package, or warpage data.
- No more than 4 active logic tiers; "tall Z full of active logic" is contraindicated.
- No wafer-to-wafer (W2W) bonding for the stack (cannot sort KGD).
- No fine per-PE logic fold without a sequential-integration (monolithic-3D) PDK.
